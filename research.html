Research

Research


Our interests lie in the field of cellular neurobiology. We investigate the processes of axonal cargo biogenesis and cargo transport and their impact on neuronal function and behaviour of the organism, with implications for neurodegenerative diseases.


Current Projects in Lab


Regulation of pre-synaptic vesicle transport :

Cloning and charcterization of suppressors and enhancers of the synaptic vesicle motor mutant unc-104.
Understanding the role LRK-1 in synaptic vesicle protein trafficking.

Regulation of mitochondrial transport and distribution in neurons. :

Role of calcium in vivo mitochondrial transport regulation.
Understanding the contribution of regulated neuronal distributions of mitochondria to behaviour.

Developing new in vivo tools to help us image cargo transport in neurons. Many of these studies are carried out collaboratively :

Developing PDMS platforms to carry out functional imaging studies.
Developing methods to use PI(4,5)P2 sensors in vivo.
We are interested in extending the repertoire of methologies available to study transport in vivo in the C. elegans model.

Quantitative understanding of vesicle transport :

Developing mathematical models to explain observed experimental features of axonal transport.
Determining the 'transport' phenotypes of key mutants in axonal transport to serve as a benchmark for the simulations.

About Lab

Our lab is interested in linking cell biology of the neuron to function and behaviour of the organism. We focus on how transport of axonal cargo is regulated, using C. elegans as a model organism. Using a combination of classical genetics, molecular & biochemical approaches and in vivo imaging techniques, our lab investigates synaptic vesicle protein trafficking, regulation of cargo-motor interactions, axoplasmic crowding and regulation of cargo distribution along the neuronal process.
